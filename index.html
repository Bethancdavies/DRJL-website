Neuroscientist

About

I am a cellular and behavioural neuroscientist and educator, interested in studying brain repair strategies following injury to the central nervous system. Using various injury models, my research aims to improve functional recovery using pharmacological, cellular reprogramming, and rehabilitative approaches.

The brain has the fascinating ability to produce new neurons and other cell types after injury, but these responses are insufficient for functional repair. I try to figure out how we might better engage these natural responses to improve and accelerate regeneration, with the goal of finding novel therapeutic strategies.

I have lots of in vivo and in vitro experience, and know lots of molecular and surgical techniques, and behavioural assays to test motor and cognitive function. I am particularly passionate about scientific writing and communication, and I love collaborations!

Skills

In vivo, I have years of experience working with rats and mice- including breeding and maintaining transgenic lines. I regularly use preclinical models of stroke, cerebral palsy, and multiple sclerosis. I have lots of surgical experience, and am an expert in behavioural testing!

In vitro, I have experience working with primary and immortalized cell cultures, growing organoids, performing neurosphere assays, and microfluidic cultures. I also have experience with in vitro injury models like oxygen-glucose deprivation.

Molecular techniques, I use molecular techniques including protein and RNA analysis to determine regulation and expression of proteins, histology to investigate gross changes on a cellular and tissue level, and immunoflouresence to identify cells and proteins.

Communication, I love science communication, and believe that science literacy is something everyone benefits from. In addition to academic talks and publications, I have engaged in science communication with community groups and schools. I also work as a freelance scientific editor for a global communication company!
